ARIMA Machine Learning Model


Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average

ARIMA models are a general class of models used for forecasting time series data. ARIMA models use differencing to convert a non-stationary time series into a stationary one, and then predict future values from historical data.These models use “auto” correlations and moving averages over residual errors in the data to forecast future values.
Pros
    Only requires the prior data of a time series to generalize the forecast
    Performs well on short term forecasts
    Models non-stationary time series
Cons
    Difficult to predict turning points
    There is quite a bit of subjectivity involved in determining (p,d,q) order of the model.
    Computationally expensive
    Poorer performance for long term forecasts
    Cannot be used for seasonal time series
